# Design-partner outreach — DM variants

Three message variants targeting three PMM personas. Goal: 5 design
partners signed up by end of week 2.

The ask is concrete — a 30-minute concierge session where you run their
real upcoming launch through Drumbeat *for them* and they walk away with
an actual launch package. That's the value exchange.

---

## Variant A — Solo PMM at a B2B SaaS startup

**Channel:** LinkedIn DM. Best for: PMMs who are "Head of Product
Marketing" at companies under 200 people, often the only PMM.

> Hi {Name} — saw you're owning PM marketing solo at {Company}. I've
> been there and I know the launch grind: positioning, messaging matrix,
> sales enablement, blog, internal comms, social — all on you, all under
> deadline.
>
> I'm building **Drumbeat**, an AI tool that turns a product brief into
> a tier-classified launch package (6 assets, ~30 minutes). Built on the
> PMA methodology I learned in their certification.
>
> Before I write a line of app code, I want to run 5 real PMM launches
> through it manually and see what works. **Would you be up for a
> 30-min session where I take a launch you have coming up and produce
> the full asset pack live?** You keep everything, no obligation.
>
> Worth a quick chat?

**Hook:** the "solo PMM grind" pain.
**Value:** real assets for a real launch.
**Friction:** 30 min, no obligation.

## Variant B — PMM lead at a growth-stage company (50–500 people)

**Channel:** LinkedIn DM, PMA Slack, or warm intro. Best for: PMM
managers / leads who own quarterly launch quality across a team.

> Hi {Name} — I noticed you lead PMM at {Company}. Quick question: when
> your team ships a launch, how consistent is the quality from launch to
> launch? In my experience, even strong PMM teams reinvent the wheel
> every quarter — different brief format, different messaging structure,
> different asset coverage.
>
> I'm prototyping **Drumbeat** — an opinionated launch workflow that
> bakes in the PMA-style methodology so every launch comes out of one
> consistent process: brief → tier classification → messaging matrix →
> 6-asset pack → hub the team can edit together.
>
> I'm running 5 design-partner sessions in the next two weeks where I
> walk a real launch through the workflow with you and one of your PMMs.
> 45 minutes, you get a complete asset pack to use, and I get the
> feedback I need to scope v1.
>
> Up for it? I can send a calendar link if so.

**Hook:** consistency / quality across a team.
**Value:** asset pack + a process worth stealing.
**Friction:** 45 min, two people.

## Variant C — Founder doing PMM themselves

**Channel:** LinkedIn DM, indie founder Slacks, X DM. Best for: solo
founders / early-stage CEOs without a PMM hire.

> Hi {Name} — I see you're heads-down on {Product}. Curious: when you
> ship a feature or launch, what's your messaging process? Most founders
> I talk to wing it on launch comms because PMM isn't their domain — and
> the result is usually a thin blog post and a single tweet.
>
> I'm building **Drumbeat** — give it a 1-page product brief and it
> produces a launch package using the same methodology PMMs at companies
> like Atlassian and Notion use (Launch Tiering, Messaging Framework,
> Story Toolkit). 6 assets out of one brief in.
>
> I want to run it manually with 5 founders before I build the
> self-serve product. **30 minutes, you bring something you're about to
> launch, I produce the full package live, you keep it.** No catch —
> just need the feedback.
>
> Interested?

**Hook:** "you're winging launch comms because PMM isn't your domain."
**Value:** PMM-grade launch package for a real launch.
**Friction:** 30 min, no obligation.

## Sending playbook

- **Don't blast.** Pick 30–50 named targets. Personalize the first line
  (mention their company / a recent launch / a post they wrote).
- **Track in a sheet.** Columns: name, company, channel, sent date,
  reply, scheduled, completed, would-pay (Y/N), notes.
- **Aim for 10–15% reply rate.** That's ~5 sessions from ~40 sends.
- **Cap follow-ups at 1.** A polite "bumping this — still interested?"
  after 5 days. After that, drop it.
- **After every session,** log: what they loved, what they edited
  heavily, what they asked for that didn't exist, would they pay (and
  how much).

## Questions to ask in every session

1. Walk me through your last 3 launches — what was painful?
2. What's the first thing you'd cut from your launch process if you could?
3. What tools do you use today? (Notion, Asana, Jasper, …)
4. If this existed today as a paid tool, what would you pay per month?
5. What would have to be true for you to switch from your current
   workflow to this?

## Exit criteria

≥5 PMMs / founders complete a session. ≥3 say "I'd pay $X/mo for this
if it were self-serve." If fewer than 3 say yes, **stop and re-examine
the wedge** before writing app code.

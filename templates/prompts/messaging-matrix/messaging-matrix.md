# Messaging matrix — Messaging Framework applied

Generate a messaging matrix grounded in the brief and the tier. The
matrix is the single source of truth that every downstream asset draws
from — get it right and the assets are easy.

## Output structure

A markdown table with these columns:

| Audience | Pain / Job-to-be-Done | Core message | Proof point | Best channel |

Plus a short prose block underneath naming:

- The **anchor message** — the one sentence the entire launch reduces to
- The **tagline candidates** — 2–3 short variants for headlines and hero
  copy
- **What this launch is NOT about** — the easy mistakes the messaging
  must avoid

## How to derive each row

### Audience
3–5 rows. Cover at minimum:

- **Decision maker** — the person who approves spend (CFO, VP, founder)
- **Influencer / champion** — the person who recommends adoption (PMM
  lead, head of growth)
- **End user** — the person who uses the product daily (PMM, ops)

Add additional rows for distinct sub-segments only if the brief gives
real reason to (e.g., "we sell to both startups and enterprise — the
buying center differs").

### Pain / Job-to-be-Done
The job they hire the product to do, in their words. Not the feature.

✅ "Ship launch comms in days instead of weeks without sacrificing
quality."
❌ "Use AI to streamline marketing workflows."

### Core message
One sentence. Specific. No generic phrases.

✅ "Drumbeat turns a product brief into 6 launch assets in 30 minutes,
on PMM-grade methodology your team can defend."
❌ "Drumbeat is the AI-powered launch platform that empowers teams."

The core message must contain:
- The audience-relevant outcome
- The mechanism (briefly)
- The differentiator (implicit or explicit)

### Proof point
A specific, verifiable claim. If the brief doesn't supply one, write
`[PROOF NEEDED — what kind]` so the user can fill it in.

✅ "10 design partners cut average launch prep from 6 weeks to 9 days."
✅ "[PROOF NEEDED — beta customer quote on time savings]"
❌ "Trusted by leading companies."

### Best channel
Where this audience actually pays attention. Examples: founder LinkedIn,
sales-led email, CS in-app banner, partner co-marketing, paid LinkedIn,
SEO content. Match channel to audience, not to "what we have."

## Anchor message — how to write it

The anchor message answers: "If a customer remembers exactly one thing
from this launch, what should it be?" It feeds the blog headline, the
sales pitch, and the social hooks.

Format:
> {Product} {does the unique thing} for {audience} so they can {outcome
> they care about}.

Example:
> Drumbeat turns product briefs into PMM-grade launch packages for
> product marketers so they can ship launches in days instead of weeks.

## Tagline candidates

2–3 short variants (5–10 words each). Test different angles:

1. **Outcome-first** — "Ship every launch in days."
2. **Mechanism-first** — "From brief to launch package in 30 minutes."
3. **Methodology-first** — "PMM methodology, AI execution."

## Quality checks

Before delivering the matrix, verify:

- [ ] Every Core message is unique to this brief (would not work for a
      generic competitor)
- [ ] No banned generic phrases ("industry-leading," "best-in-class,"
      "robust," "seamless," "synergy")
- [ ] Proof points are specific or marked `[PROOF NEEDED]`
- [ ] Audience rows reflect real decision-making units, not invented
      personas
- [ ] Anchor message survives the "would the competitor's product also
      satisfy this sentence?" test

If any check fails, rewrite before showing the user.

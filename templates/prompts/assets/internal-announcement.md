# Asset — Internal launch announcement

A single message that brings the company along. Goes in Slack
`#announcements`, email, or all-hands deck.

## Inputs
- Brief
- Tier
- Messaging matrix (especially anchor message)

## Output structure

```
**{Product / feature name}** is launching {date}.

**What it is.** {One sentence — anchor message, internal voice.}

**Why now.** {Strategic context — what this unlocks for the company.
Cite the OKR or initiative this serves.}

**Who it's for.** {Primary audience from the messaging matrix.}

**The headline number.** {Most important proof point or expected
outcome — be honest, no hype.}

**What every team should know.**
- Sales: {1-line implication for sales pitch / pipeline}
- CS / Support: {1-line implication for customer conversations}
- Marketing: {1-line implication for content / campaigns}
- Eng / Product: {1-line implication for what's shipped vs. coming}

**Resources.** {Links to: full launch brief, sales one-pager, blog
draft, FAQ. Use placeholders like `{LINK: sales-one-pager}` for the
user to fill in.}

**Questions?** {Owner name + Slack handle from the brief.}
```

## Length
- Tier 1: 200–250 words
- Tier 2: 150–200 words
- Tier 3: 80–120 words

## Tone
Direct, confident, not hyped. This is internal — no marketing speak.
The internal team will mock you if you write "we are thrilled and
excited to announce." Just say what's shipping and why it matters.

## Quality criteria

- [ ] One-sentence "what it is" survives the elevator test
- [ ] "Why now" cites a real strategic priority, not a generic "we
      believe" sentence
- [ ] Each team callout is specific to that team's job
- [ ] No internal jargon that excludes new hires
- [ ] No banned generic phrases

## Common failure modes

- **Burying the lede.** Open with what's launching, not background.
- **Marketing voice internally.** Internal teams want operational facts,
  not press-release energy.
- **Skipping team callouts.** "Sales should know X" is the difference
  between an announcement and an enabling message.

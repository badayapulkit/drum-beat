# Asset — Sales script talking points

Discovery questions, pitch frame, and objection handlers a sales rep
uses live on a call. Not a verbatim script — a structured cheat sheet.

## Inputs
- Brief
- Tier (skip this asset for Tier 3 unless sales relevance is high)
- Messaging matrix
- Sales one-pager (objections + competitors)

## Output structure

```
# {Product} — sales call talking points

## Pre-call — 3-minute prep
- Who is the prospect? {Title, company stage, industry — pulled from
  ICP in the messaging matrix}
- What's likely on their mind? {2–3 hypotheses about their current
  pain, drawn from the messaging matrix Pain column}
- What signal qualifies / disqualifies this deal? {1–2 signals}

## Discovery — open the call
Ask 3–5 of these. Listen for the pain you can solve.

1. {Question — pulled from the ICP's likely pain. Specific, not generic
   "tell me about your priorities."}
2. {Question}
3. {Question}
4. {Question}
5. {Question}

## Pitch — when you have the opening (60–90 seconds)

**Hook.** {One sentence — anchor message in customer voice, framed as
their problem solved.}

**Mechanism.** {2–3 sentences — how it works in plain language. Use a
concrete example tied to what they just told you.}

**Proof.** {1–2 sentences — specific customer / metric. If `[PROOF
NEEDED]`, prep the rep to use the closest available.}

**The ask.** {What you want next — demo, technical eval, intro to a
team member. Match to deal stage.}

## Objection handlers

For each, the rep gets: the objection, what's actually being said
underneath, and the response.

**1. "{Objection}"**
- Underneath: {What the prospect probably means}
- Response: {2–3 sentences. Acknowledge, reframe, evidence.}

**2. "{Objection}"**
- Underneath: {…}
- Response: {…}

**3. "{Objection}"**
- Underneath: {…}
- Response: {…}

**4. "{Objection}"**
- Underneath: {…}
- Response: {…}

**5. "{Objection}"**
- Underneath: {…}
- Response: {…}

## Competitive callouts (only if asked)

If prospect mentions {Competitor}: {1–2 sentence honest contrast. Where
they win, where you win.}

If prospect mentions {Competitor 2}: {…}

## Closing the call

- Confirm the next step in writing during the call.
- Set a calendar invite before hanging up.
- Send a follow-up within 2 hours: thank-you, recap, next-step
  confirmation, attached one-pager.

## Trap questions to expect

{2–3 questions that come up that aren't objections per se but that reps
fumble: "Where will you be in 12 months?" / "Why should I bet on you
vs. an incumbent?" / "What if you get acquired?" Each with a 1-sentence
response.}
```

## Length
2 pages max. Reps reference this live; long is unusable.

## Tone
Operational. Bullet-heavy. The rep should be able to scan it during a
silence and find the next question or response in 3 seconds.

## Quality criteria

- [ ] Discovery questions are specific to this product's sweet spot,
      not generic SaaS discovery
- [ ] Each objection handler names what's *underneath* the objection
      before responding
- [ ] Competitive callouts are honest (where competitor wins, too)
- [ ] "Trap questions" section is included — these separate strong
      reps from average ones
- [ ] CTA / next-step language is concrete

## Common failure modes

- **Generic discovery.** "Walk me through your current process" is
  table stakes; the script should add 3 questions a rep wouldn't
  already ask.
- **Defensive objection handling.** Don't argue the prospect out of
  their concern; acknowledge it first.
- **No "trap questions" section.** This is what makes a sales script
  feel valuable to senior reps.

# Asset — Sales one-pager / FAQ

The single document a sales rep references in a deal. Lives in
enablement tools (Highspot, Notion, Drive). Must answer the questions a
prospect actually asks.

## Inputs
- Brief
- Tier
- Messaging matrix
- Differentiation + competitive context from brief

## Output structure

```
# {Product / feature name} — sales one-pager

## What it is
{Two sentences. First sentence = anchor message in customer voice.
Second sentence = mechanism — how it works, without engineering depth.}

## Who it's for
{2–3 bullet points naming the ICP — role, company stage, problem
context. Specific enough that a rep can disqualify a bad fit in 30
seconds.}

## Problem we solve
{2–3 bullet points naming the pain in customer language. Each bullet
should make a target customer nod.}

## How it works (in 3 steps)
1. {Step 1 — customer-facing, not engineering-facing}
2. {Step 2}
3. {Step 3}

## Proof
- {Customer / metric 1 — specific or `[PROOF NEEDED]`}
- {Customer / metric 2}
- {Customer / metric 3}

## Pricing
{One-line summary. Link to pricing page. If pricing is sensitive, write
"Contact sales — see playbook."}

## Top 5 objections + responses

**1. "{Objection — phrased as a customer would say it}"**
{2–3 sentence response. Acknowledge, reframe, evidence. No hand-waving.}

**2. "{Objection 2}"**
{Response.}

**3. "{Objection 3}"**
{Response.}

**4. "{Objection 4}"**
{Response.}

**5. "{Objection 5}"**
{Response.}

## Top competitors

| Competitor | Where they win | Where we win |
|---|---|---|
| {Name 1} | {Honest assessment} | {Honest assessment} |
| {Name 2} | {…} | {…} |

If the brief lists no competitors, write `[COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
NEEDED]` and ask the user to provide.

## Discovery questions
{3–5 questions a rep should ask early in a call to qualify the deal and
position the product. Not generic discovery — specific to this product's
sweet spot.}

## Where this product is NOT a fit
{2–3 honest disqualifiers. Builds rep trust + prevents bad-fit deals.}
```

## Length
1–2 pages printed. Sales reps will not read more.

## Tone
Customer-voice for the "what / who / problem" sections. Direct and
candid for the objections, competitors, and "not a fit" sections.
Reps need a tool, not a brochure.

## Quality criteria

- [ ] Anchor message is in customer language, not internal jargon
- [ ] Objections are real — pulled from win/loss feedback if available,
      not invented
- [ ] Competitor table is honest about where competitors win
- [ ] Discovery questions disqualify, not just qualify
- [ ] "Not a fit" section is present and specific

## Common failure modes

- **Marketing copy where reps need facts.** A rep mid-call needs the
  data point, not the brand voice.
- **Pretending no one wins where competitors do.** Reps get burned in
  bake-offs; honesty pre-empts the burn.
- **Generic objection responses.** "We have great support" is not a
  response to "your pricing is too high."

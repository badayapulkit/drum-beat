# Asset — Blog post draft

The launch-day blog post on the company's site. Lives at /blog or /news.
Must be skimmable, quotable, and SEO-aware.

## Inputs
- Brief
- Tier
- Messaging matrix (especially anchor message and proof points)
- Story Toolkit (problem → stakes → solution → proof structure)

## Output structure

```
# {Headline — anchor message tightened to <70 characters, news-style}

**{Subhead — one sentence elaborating the headline. Sets the value prop
explicitly.}**

{Hero paragraph — 3–4 sentences. Lead with the customer problem, not
the product. State the stakes. Land on what's launching today.}

## The problem we're solving
{2–3 paragraphs. Customer voice. Concrete examples. Cite data if the
brief supplies it.}

## Why this is hard today
{1–2 paragraphs. What workarounds exist. Why they're insufficient. This
is where you earn credibility — show that you understand the customer's
current world before pitching the new one.}

## What we built
{2–3 paragraphs. Walk through the product capability. Use 1–2 short
example scenarios — concrete, not abstract. Avoid feature-list format.}

## What customers are seeing
{1–2 paragraphs of proof. Customer quote (verbatim or `[PROOF NEEDED —
quote from beta customer about X]`). Specific metric. If neither
exists, this section is short and honest: "We're launching this with N
design partners and will publish results in M weeks."}

## What's next
{1 paragraph. Roadmap signal — where this is heading, what to expect in
the next 1–2 quarters. Concrete enough to be useful, vague enough to
preserve flexibility.}

## How to get started
{1 paragraph + CTA. Free trial / pricing link / book-a-demo link as
relevant. Match CTA to the buyer journey for the audience.}

---

**SEO metadata**

- Title tag (≤60 chars): {…}
- Meta description (≤155 chars): {…}
- Primary keyword: {…}
- Secondary keywords: {2–3}
- Suggested URL slug: {/blog/…}
- Internal links to add: {2–3 existing pages this post should link to}
```

## Length
- Tier 1: 800–1200 words
- Tier 2: 500–800 words
- Tier 3: short release note (200–400 words) — use a different
  structure: lead, what changed, who it's for, link to docs

## Tone
Confident and clear. Customer-first framing. No "we are excited to
announce" — open on the problem instead. Quotable sentences scattered
through (subhead-worthy lines that a journalist or social poster could
lift).

## Quality criteria

- [ ] Headline survives the "no jargon, news desk" test
- [ ] Hero paragraph leads with customer problem, not product
- [ ] At least one quotable, specific sentence per major section
- [ ] No banned generic phrases
- [ ] Proof section is honest (real quote/data OR honest beta framing)
- [ ] CTA matches the audience and buyer-journey stage
- [ ] SEO metadata is filled in, not skipped

## Common failure modes

- **Press-release voice.** "Today, {Company} is proud to announce…" —
  rewrite to lead with the customer problem.
- **Feature-list paragraphs.** Replace with example scenarios.
- **Vague proof.** "Customers love it" is worse than no proof — it
  signals you have nothing real.
- **No SEO metadata.** Post will underperform out of the gate.

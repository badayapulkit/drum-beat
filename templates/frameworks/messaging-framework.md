# Framework — Messaging Framework

Derivative description of audience-segmented messaging methodology
taught in PMM programs. Our own words.

## The idea

Different audiences hire your product for different reasons. A CFO
cares about cost and risk. A PMM lead cares about consistency and
career credit. An end-user PMM cares about getting their Friday-night
launch off their plate.

A messaging framework derives a *unique core message* per audience from
your positioning, paired with a proof point and a channel where that
audience actually pays attention.

## The matrix

| Audience | Pain / Job-to-be-Done | Core message | Proof point | Best channel |
|---|---|---|---|---|

3–5 rows is typical. Each row reflects a real decision-making unit, not
an invented persona.

## How to derive each column

### Audience
The role + context. "VP of Marketing at a 200–500 person SaaS
company." Specific enough that you could find ten of them on LinkedIn.

### Pain / Job-to-be-Done
The job the audience hires the product to do, in their words. The Job
to be Done framing matters: people don't buy products, they hire them
to make progress on something they already care about.

### Core message
One sentence. Specific. Derives from positioning. Different for each
audience.

### Proof point
The most credible piece of evidence for that audience. Different
audiences trust different proof:

- Decision makers trust ROI data and peer logos
- Influencers trust expert endorsement and methodology
- End users trust before/after demos and testimonials from people like
  them

### Best channel
Where the audience actually pays attention. Decision makers: founder
LinkedIn, sales-led email, conference talks. End users: in-product,
peer communities (Slack, Reddit), YouTube tutorials.

## Anchor message

Beneath the matrix, name the *anchor message* — the single sentence
the entire launch reduces to. Every asset draws from this. If you
can't write the anchor message in one sentence, the launch positioning
is too vague.

## Common mistakes

- **Same message, different audience.** If every row of the matrix
  reads the same with the audience name swapped, you haven't done the
  work.
- **Generic proof.** "Trusted by leading companies" is worse than no
  proof — it signals you have nothing real.
- **Inventing personas.** Audience rows must reflect the actual buying
  center for this product, not generic marketing personas.
- **Channel = "social."** Pick the specific channel and the specific
  format (LinkedIn long-form post, not "social posts").

## How Drumbeat uses the messaging framework

The messaging matrix is the second AI step in the workflow — between
tier classification and asset generation. Every downstream asset
(blog, sales one-pager, customer email, social) draws from the matrix.
This is what keeps the assets *consistent* with each other instead of
sounding like six different freelancers wrote them.
